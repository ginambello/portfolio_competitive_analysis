This is an html competitive analysis portfolio gathering 10 exemplar website designs and analyzing them by the following design criteria: Brand, Functionality, Interactivity, and Navigation.

A Look At: The New Yorker

Website # Site Name Url Layout Type Date Looked At
1 The New Yorker http://www.newyorker.com/ Power Grid Tuesday 17. January . 2012
Design Criteria Analysis
Brand

Sophisticated. A simple white based background. Minimal color except in the ads and the features. The audience is not the average magazine reader. Flaboyant images do not dominate the fold. The distinct style of this webpage is defined by the typography. Notice the "W" in The New Yorker. Although there is a cartoon style, the cartoons revolve around political/artisitc/etc figures rather than the chitter-chatter of popular tabloid rags. Pay attention to how well-dressed the charater in the logo is; this isn't your average Joe. It gives you an idea of the intended reader of this magazine.

Examiner. Take a look at the upper left logo cartoon. The charater has a spectacle examining the text "The New Yorker". The spectacle is a symbol of "critique". It symbolizes examining the news with a critical eye. The feature articles surround the US election, Thatcher, outsourcing with opinions on the ongoings in congress and international affairs.

American. The dominant colors are white, red, and blue. The heading "The New Yorker" alludes that New York is America, furthermore that New Yorkers are the 'better' Americans. It plays with this idea that New Yorkers are the most informed, therefore bringing you the news on America.
Functionality The site is used to promote subscription to this magazine. You can subscribe by selecting "Subcribe" in the topbar section of the site and also by selecting "Subscribe" in the upper right corner. The option to subscribe is not only located in the fold you can also subscribe below the fold. The commodities are style and news. To do this the site displays top news in containers within the page. The images like the cartoons and the typography are in essence the shiny things in the window display; it's what draws you into this magazine.
Interactivity The interactivity is simple. The basic search engine to type a desired entry. Then there is a top gallery that allows you to navigate through 5 images. Though this coverflow type gallery is timed you can move through the images as well by clicking on the next gray dot in the series.
Navigation There are 7 main places to go within the site. They are: in the magazine, blogs, audio&video, listings, cartoons, topics and archive. The link to these pages are in the top bar. Underneath this topbar is a sub-topbar that holds content specific to each of the 7 pages. Let's say for example that you are trying click on blogs. You will go to the blogs page, but once in the the blogs page it is difficult to differentiate between in the magazine and blogs pages since the set up is the same. There is however a little light gray header on the left above the cover flow gallery that says "The New Yorker | Magazine" or "The New Yorker | Online Only". The user is supposed to make the link that "Online Only" means that you are in the blogs page. This is a little confusing, but then again you can look at the web address and it will tell you "http://www.newyorker.com/online/blogs". To go back to the home page you can click on the left logo, which consists of the cartoon and the "The New Yorker" text.

A Look At: Valet

Website # Site Name Url Layout Type Date Looked At
2 Valet http://www.valetmag.com/ Advanced Grid Wednesday 18. January . 2012
Design Criteria Analysis
Brand

Gentleman Style. The site gives an air of quality and sophistication. This site focus on the look of the ideal gentleman. The word style denotes an approach. For example the site has a whole page on grooming with style. A guy can use a plastic razor to shave or a uniquely crafted metal razor with with special shaving cream and horse hair brush.

Detailed. The way in which a gentleman approaches life is by paying attention to the details of his manner. From the type of shoes to the hanckerchief in his suit pocket. A gentleman knows the type of suit he is wearing and the type of shoe. Among his worries is the cuff link he will pair with his french dress shirt. This site has images illustrating various belt styles, how to tie a bow tie, etc. because they are details that gentlemen pay attention to and style.

Adivsory. This site is built as an advise platform. In short it is selling a way of life. In the featured containers are links to how to pick dress shirts, how to get a suit tailored, how to pronounce designer names accurately. There is a container dedicated to asking Valet for advise. The site columnists answer the submissions and make suggests. There are posts on what to buy, but what sets this site apart is that they tell you how to style the things you buy.
Functionality The site is used to teach and promote style. It accomplishes this by playing the role of a valet. A valet is man's personal male attendant, responsible for his clothes and appearance. The site has 4 basic duties a the users' personal valet: personal shopper, style adivsor, groomer, and daily living advisor. Each post on the site fits in that category. If there is something that is not highlighted by an existing post, the website invites question submissions. There is an Ask Valet page, that has a collection of posed and answered questions with images to accompany the explanations. The key is that Valet is run by experts from style guros to designers to style enthusiasts and they are your personal valet. So at your hand you have experts telling you how to be stylish.
Interactivity The interactivity is simple click on image and you go to the artcle regarding that image. There is a nice feature on this site that allows the user to view the information on the main page in 2 ways. One is by sorting by featured articles or by most recently posted article. On the top left of the first container gallery are 2 small gray boxes that enable sorting. The one with boxes displays the articles by thumbnail views, the box below it has lines and that displays the articles in an RSS feed manner. This view is dataed and time stamped.
Navigation Under the page header is the topbar and you can navigate through the site in that manner. Clicking on: Style, Grooming, and Living. Within the grid of the main page the three bottom right columns follow the above set-up: Stylem, Grooming, and Living. There is no sub-topbar. To get back home you click on the top left yellow highlighted animation. Also, in the footer site map is Style, Grooming and Living.

A Look At: Guberland

Website # Site Name Url Layout Type Date Looked At
3 Guberland http://www.matthewgraygubler.com/ Full Screen Photo Wednesday 18. January . 2012
Design Criteria Analysis
Brand

Playful. The site creates a fantasy world in one featured animated graphic. There are dinosaur looking monsters walking through the image. The creatures in the image are ugly looking but they are not harmful looking nor disgusting. They are intriguing and interact with the user in a curious manner. The frog thinks he is a horse. Another creature is French and the ghosts float around the mountain as the cho-cho train passes by the tracks. It is playful as in a child's creation/imagination.

Unique. These monsters exist no where else but on this site. It creates an environment for the user, moreover a fictional one, a place that does not exist in our lives. Because this image is hand-drawn it is one of a kind. It is an artist proof that is not replicable.

Messy. Lines in the image are hand-drawn and therefore not straight. There are no guidlines guiding the inside of the image, it is free-hand completely. The logo: "Greetings From.. GUBLER-land" is slanted downward creating a droppy effect. The cho-cho tracks have jagged lines and some lines look redrawn on top of another. The text is wavy with different font types, it breaks the order much like imagination does.
Functionality The site is used to self promote tv star, Matthew Gray Gubler, as an artist. The site functions as a creative portfolio. It does not consist of a list of his cameos rather it serves as a gallery of his work from sketches to videos. It aims at selling a personality/ person because it is a personally created website, not a marketing tool for getting acting jobs.
Interactivity The interactivity is esstential for the brand characteristic-"playful". As one loads the page the monster walks by and if you click on the monsters on the bottom they each have a distinct response to a click. By clicking on some parts of the image you get sound effects, other parts produce movement effects.
Navigation The navigation between sites is embedded in the image. There are banners spanning across the top
